Marie Curie

Marie Curie was a Polish-born physicist and chemist who conducted pioneering research on radioactivity. She was the first woman to win a Nobel Prize and the only person to win Nobel Prizes in two different scientific fields.

Early life

Marie Curie was born Maria Skłodowska in Warsaw. She showed an early aptitude for science but faced significant barriers to higher education because she was a woman.

Education

Curie moved to Paris in 1891 to study at the University of Paris, where she earned degrees in physics and mathematics.
